About KYMA

Kenya Youth Mentorship Alliance - Inspiring and empowering Kenyan youth to become confident, ethical, and visionary leaders

Our Vision & Mission

Vision

To inspire and empower Kenyan youth and students to become confident, ethical, and visionary leaders who positively shape their communities and the nation.

Mission

KYMA exists to provide mentorship, guidance, and opportunities for youth and students to grow in character, leadership, innovation, and career readiness through structured programs, partnerships, and community engagement.

Our Core Values

Integrity
Service

Our Objectives

  • Empower Kenyan youth with mentorship and leadership skills
  • Build strong partnerships with schools, universities, and industries
  • Promote values, character development, and national identity
  • Support youth in entrepreneurship, innovation, and career readiness
  • Foster community service, spiritual growth, and social responsibility

Our Programs

Mentorship & Guidance

Peer-to-peer and professional mentorship programs to guide youth in their personal and professional development.

Leadership Development

Training, bootcamps, and forums designed to cultivate the next generation of student leaders.

Academic & Career Empowerment

Career guidance, internship opportunities, and scholarship support to help youth achieve their academic goals.

Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Startup support, financial literacy programs, and innovation hubs to foster entrepreneurial mindsets.

Life Skills & Wellness

Communication training, digital literacy, and mental health support for holistic youth development.

Community Service

Environmental conservation initiatives, charity work, and volunteerism opportunities.

Talent Development

Competitions, arts programs, sports activities, and STEM promotion to nurture diverse talents.

Our Structure

National Level

Led by the National Executive Council (NEC) with a President/CEO, Deputy President, Secretary General, National Treasurer, and Program Directors overseeing various initiatives.

County Level

County Directors and Executive Committees coordinate programs and activities at the county level across Kenya.

Institutional Level

KYMA Clubs established in schools and universities, led by student leaders with guidance from faculty patrons.

Membership Categories

Student Members

Pupils, high school, college, and university students actively engaged in KYMA programs and activities.

Youth Members

Out-of-school youth aged 18-35 years committed to personal growth and community impact.

Institutional Members

Schools, universities, or organizations formally registered with KYMA to support youth development.

Honorary Members

Individuals recognized for their outstanding contribution to mentorship and youth empowerment.
